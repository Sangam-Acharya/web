Planets (ग्रह)

Sun(Surya)

The Sun is considered the King of the planets, representing the soul or Atma in Vedic astrology. It is the source of life, vitality, and energy. The Sun governs self-expression, ego, and authority. It defines a person’s individuality and confidence. People with a strong Sun in their chart are often seen as natural leaders and are typically motivated by a desire to shine and be noticed. It also influences health, especially related to the heart and overall vitality. A strong Sun brings success and recognition, while a weak Sun can lead to issues with self-esteem or authority.

Moon (Chandra):

The Moon represents the mind, emotions, and instincts. It is the ruler of feelings, nurturing, and mental well-being. The Moon also governs motherhood, femininity, and the home environment. It reflects how one processes emotions, their inner peace, and mental stability. The Moon controls the mind’s fluctuations and is crucial for understanding emotional health. A strong Moon brings emotional balance and a nurturing nature, while a weak Moon can lead to mood swings, mental instability, or a lack of emotional connection.

Mars (Mangal):

Mars represents action, drive, and courage. It governs physical strength, ambition, and assertiveness. Known for its aggressive and fiery nature, Mars influences how one approaches challenges and takes initiative. It is associated with warriors, sports, and competition. A well-placed Mars provides the energy to overcome obstacles and take bold actions, while a poorly placed Mars can lead to impulsiveness, conflict, and aggression. It also governs the sexual energy and assertiveness in relationships.

Mercury (Budh):

Mercury represents intellect, communication, and reasoning. It is the planet of wisdom, learning, and technology. Mercury governs how we process information, communicate, and express ideas. It also rules business, commerce, and writing. People with a strong Mercury are usually good at debating, teaching, and problem-solving. A strong Mercury gives sharpness of mind, clarity in speech, and success in intellectual pursuits, while a weak Mercury can lead to confusion, miscommunication, or difficulties in learning and understanding.

Jupiter (Guru):

Jupiter is known as the great benefic and is the planet of expansion, wisdom, and good fortune. It governs higher knowledge, spirituality, and morality. Jupiter is associated with education, philosophy, religion, and long-term planning. It brings luck, growth, and success in matters related to career, wealth, and health. A strong Jupiter fosters a sense of optimism, generosity, and philosophical wisdom, leading to spiritual growth and prosperity. However, a poorly placed Jupiter can result in overindulgence, excess, or a lack of direction in life.
